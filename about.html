The Origin of Our Fiduciary Promise

Prior to launching Aurelius, our founding team spent over two decades in major institutional brokerage firms. We witnessed firsthand how corporate sales quotas, proprietary product pushing, and hidden commission structures compromised client outcomes.

We believed there was a better way—a structure where advice is paid for, not investment products. In 2012, we transitioned to a fee-only, Registered Investment Advisor (RIA) model. By operating under a fiduciary standard, we are legally bound to act in our clients' best interest in all scenarios.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a fiduciary and why does it matter?
A fiduciary is legal professional bound by the highest legal and ethical obligations to act solely in the best interests of their clients. Brokers, conversely, are held to a lower "suitability" standard, allowing them to sell higher-fee products if they fit your general profile. As fiduciaries, we must disclose all fees, avoid conflicts of interest, and seek best-execution.
How does your "fee-only" pricing work?
Our advisory services are paid for through a simple Asset Under Management (AUM) billing percentage (typically starting at 1.00% annually for initial tiers and sliding lower as portfolio size increases). We never receive sales commissions, kickbacks from funds, or referrals from custodian brokers. This aligns our financial incentives directly with the growth of your investments.
Where are my portfolio assets physically held?
Aurelius never takes physical custody of your wealth. All accounts are registered in your name and held at leading independent institutional custodians (Charles Schwab and Fidelity Clearing & Custody). You maintain ultimate control, receive statements directly from the custodian, and simply authorize Aurelius to manage allocations on your behalf.
Do you have an account minimum?
Generally, we establish accounts for families with a minimum of $500,000 in investable assets. However, we sometimes waive this minimum for young professionals or executives on high wealth-accumulation trajectories. Contact us to discuss your individual situation.
